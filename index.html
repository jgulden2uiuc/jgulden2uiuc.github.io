GDP per capita is a measure of a country's economic output for each citizen. It gives us an idea of how prosperous the country is relative to its population. This particular data comes from the World Bank's estimates according to a constant 2015 USD. With that said, GDP per capita does not take into account the cost of living within a country, nor does it tell us the typical income or quality of life for the average citizen.
Note that in the year 2020, we can clearly see the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on GDP per capita, as all countries see a slight to significant dip and subsequent rise.
